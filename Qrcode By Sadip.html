Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about QR codes

What is a QR code?
A QR code (Quick Response code) is a type of matrix barcode that contains information such as URLs, text, or other data. When scanned with a smartphone camera or QR code reader, it quickly provides access to the encoded information.
What is error correction in QR codes?
Error correction allows a QR code to be readable even if a portion of it is damaged or obscured. Higher error correction levels make the QR code more resilient but also increase its size and complexity.
How do I scan a QR code?
Most modern smartphones can scan QR codes directly through their camera app. Simply open your camera, point it at the QR code, and tap on the notification that appears. For older devices, you may need to download a dedicated QR code scanner app.
Is there a limit to how much data a QR code can store?
Yes, QR codes have data capacity limits. The maximum amount depends on the type of data and error correction level. Generally, a QR code can store up to about 4,000 alphanumeric characters or 7,000 numeric digits.
